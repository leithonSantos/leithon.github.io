What happens when older PCs get
modern updates?

Modern upgrades are often optimized for newer hardware. On older computers,
this can result in slower performance, as the hardware can't handle the
demands of the new software efficiently.

Modern upgrades tend to require more resources, such as RAM and processing
power. Older PCs, which have lower specs, may struggle to meet these
requirements, which can cause slowdowns and crashes.

Some older hardware components, such as graphics cards, network cards, or
storage drives, may not be compatible with modern drivers. This can lead to
malfunctions or lack of support for certain devices.
